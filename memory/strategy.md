# Trading Strategy — Long-Term Beat-the-S&P Experiment

**Last updated:** 2026-05-11 by Cheryl

## Strategy Type
Momentum + Fundamental — swing trading (hold days to weeks, not minutes or months).
Goal: beat the S&P 500 on a rolling 12-month basis.

## Asset Universe
- US equities only (NYSE + NASDAQ)
- Large and mid-cap (market cap > $2B)
- No penny stocks, no OTC, no leveraged ETFs

## Entry Rules (ALL must be met)
1. Stock is above its 50-day moving average (price momentum)
2. S&P 500 is above its 200-day MA (overall market not in confirmed bear trend)
3. Perplexity research finds strong fundamental story (earnings growth, sector tailwind)
4. No major earnings event in the next 3 trading days
5. No major negative news catalyst found in pre-market research

## Exit Rules
- **Stop-loss:** -7% from entry — HARD RULE, set immediately after any buy
- **Profit target:** +15-20% (scale out: sell 1/3 at +15%, 1/3 at +18%, final at +20% or trailing)
- **Time stop:** exit if thesis hasn't played out in 30 calendar days

## Position Sizing
- Max 5% of portfolio per position (~$5,000 at $100k starting capital)
- Max 10 concurrent open positions
- Max 50% of portfolio deployed at any one time (maintain cash buffer)

## Time Horizon
Swing trading — target hold period 3–30 days

## Sectors to Focus On
1. Technology (AI infrastructure, cloud, semiconductors)
2. Healthcare (biotech with catalysts, medical devices)
3. Energy transition (solar, grid, EV ecosystem)
4. Consumer discretionary (when macro is expanding)

## Indicators to Use
- Price vs 50-day MA (momentum filter)
- Price vs 200-day MA (market regime filter)
- RSI 14-day: avoid buying when RSI > 70 (overbought)
- Volume: prefer trades with above-average volume on entry day
